NOTES
Alright alright, I'll say it. The Entrepreneurs Society was my baby. Although I didn't fully realize it at the time, the Ent Soc was my first official Art Director role. When I stepped into a humdrum marketing position on the society's committee in my sophomore year, I immediately saw the need for a brand facelift. I was beginning to learn about the concept of 'graphic design' as a profession, and devouring every opportunity to create. In hindsight, these were my blastoff years. I learned how to make logos, cultivate an online presence, design for events, create videos, websites, posters...anything I could think of, I would make.

Having full creative control over an organization, however small, taught me to have confidence in my artistic abilities. Exposing myself to so many different types of design launched a career defined by learning and innovating. While these designs may not be aesthetically on par with my current work, they represent a young woman who stayed up at night teaching herself Adobe when she should have been studying economic theory. A woman who forged a career in design without any formal training, and who has enough gumption to say "in this way, I inspire myself."
